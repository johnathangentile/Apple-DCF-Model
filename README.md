# Apple-DCF-Model
Built a 5-year Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) valuation model for Apple Inc. in Microsoft Excel using historical financial statement data from Apple's SEC filings.

## Model Components
- Historical Financials
- Assumptions
- Income Statement Forecast
- Free Cash Flow Forecast
- DCF Valuation
- Sensitivity Analysis

## Key Findings
- Intrinsic Value per Share: $124.40
- Market Price at Time of Analysis: ~$295

Based on my assumptions, the DCF model estimated Apple's intrinsic value at approximately $124.2 per share. At the time of the analysis, Apple was trading around $295 per share, which is significantly higher than my valuation.

I don't believe this necessarily means the model is wrong. DCF valuations are highly dependent on the assumptions used. My model uses a WACC of 8.7%, a terminal growth rate of 2.5%, and revenue growth that gradually declines from 5% to 3% over the forecast period.

The difference between my valuation and the market price suggests investors may be expecting stronger future growth, higher profitability, a lower required return, or additional value from future products and initiatives that are not reflected in my assumptions.

The sensitivity analysis shows how much the valuation changes when WACC and terminal growth assumptions are adjusted, highlighting how important these inputs are to the final result.
